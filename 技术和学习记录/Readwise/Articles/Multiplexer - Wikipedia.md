## Highlights
- In electronics, a multiplexer (or mux; spelled sometimes as multiplexor), also known as a data selector, is a device that selects between several analog or digital input signals and forwards the selected input to a single output line.[1] The selection is directed by a separate set of digital inputs known as select lines.
